Vault: Encryption as a service

Monday, December 2, 2019

Last week I took a look at Vault’s transit secrets engine. This engine handles cryptographic functions on data-in-transit, which means that Vault doesn’t store the data sent to the secrets engine, it only encrypts or decrypts the requested data and sends it back to the requester. It can also be viewed as encryption as a service. Why would we need this? We might be interested in storing encrypted data in our application but we don’t want to deal with stuff like key rolling, authentication and auditing.
